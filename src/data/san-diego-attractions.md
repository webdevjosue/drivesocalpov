# San Diego Attractions & Landmarks Guide

## Major Tourist Attractions

### Balboa Park
- **Location**: 1549 El Prado, San Diego, CA 92101
- **Admission**: Free park entry; individual museums have varying fees ($10-25); Free Museum Tuesdays for residents
- **Hours**: Park open 24/7; Museums typically 10am-5pm
- **Highlights**:
  - 1,200-acre cultural hub with 17 museums
  - Beautiful Spanish Colonial Revival architecture
  - Gardens, walking paths, and free organ concerts on Sundays
  - Home to San Diego Zoo
- **Visitor Tips**: Check museum websites for free admission days; parking can fill up on weekends

### San Diego Zoo
- **Location**: 2920 Zoo Dr, San Diego, CA 92101 (within Balboa Park)
- **Admission**: Adults $66-76, Children (3-11) $56-66 (online discounts available)
- **Hours**: 9am-9pm (seasonal hours vary)
- **Highlights**:
  - Over 4,000 animals across 100 acres
  - World-renowned conservation programs
  - Skyfari aerial tram offers panoramic views
  - Children under 11 enter free in October
- **Visitor Tips**: Arrive early; wear comfortable shoes; consider combined zoo/safari park tickets

### USS Midway Museum
- **Location**: 910 N Harbor Dr, San Diego, CA 92101
- **Admission**: Adults $26, Youth (13-17) $18, Children (6-12) $13
- **Hours**: 10am-5pm daily
- **Highlights**:
  - Historic aircraft carrier turned museum
  - 30 restored aircraft and interactive exhibits
  - Flight simulators and audio tours
  - Spectacular harbor views
- **Visitor Tips**: Allow 3-4 hours for full visit; audio tour included with admission

## Free Attractions & Points of Interest

### Beaches
- **La Jolla Cove**
  - Free entry, swimming and snorkeling
  - Sea lions and seals viewing
  - Tide pools with marine life
  - Sunset viewing spot

- **La Jolla Shores Park**
  - Wide sandy beach perfect for families
  - Gentle waves ideal for beginner swimming
  - Kayak and paddleboard rentals nearby
  - Underwater Park entry point

- **Sunset Cliffs Natural Park**
  - Dramatic coastal cliffs and ocean views
  - Best sunset viewing in San Diego
  - Hiking trails along the coastline
  - Tide pools at low tide

### Urban Attractions
- **Gaslamp Quarter**
  - Historic Victorian architecture
  - Free walking tours available
  - Street performers and entertainment
  - Shopping and dining (free browsing)

- **Old Town San Diego State Historic Park**
  - Free admission to historic park
  - Mexican heritage and early California history
  - Free museums and historic buildings
  - Cultural demonstrations and events

- **Little Italy**
  - Charming neighborhood with European ambiance
  - Public art and murals
  - Farmers market (Saturdays)
  - Free cultural events throughout the year

- **San Diego Embarcadero**
  - Waterfront promenade along harbor
  - Public art installations
  - Ship watching and harbor views
  - Access to multiple attractions

## Historical Landmarks & Cultural Sites

### Cabrillo National Monument
- **Location**: 1800 Cabrillo Memorial Dr, San Diego, CA 92106
- **Admission**: $20 per vehicle (valid for 7 days)
- **Hours**: 9am-5pm daily
- **Highlights**:
  - Commemorates Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo's 1542 landing
  - Old Point Loma Lighthouse (1855)
  - Tide pools with marine life
  - Panoramic views of San Diego Bay
- **Visitor Tips**: Check tide schedules for tide pool exploration

### Mission Basilica San Diego de Alcalá
- **Location**: 10818 San Diego Mission Rd, San Diego, CA 92108
- **Admission**: Free (donations welcome)
- **Hours**: 9am-4:30pm daily
- **Highlights**:
  - First of California's 21 missions (1769)
  - Active parish and historical landmark
  - Beautiful architecture and gardens
  - Historical museum and artifacts

### Hotel del Coronado
- **Location**: 1500 Orange Ave, Coronado, CA 92118
- **Admission**: Free to visit exterior and public areas
- **Hours**: Public areas accessible during hotel hours
- **Highlights**:
  - Historic Victorian beach resort (1888)
  - Featured in movies ("Some Like It Hot")
  - Beautiful beachfront location
  - Architecture tours available
- **Visitor Tips**: Respect hotel guest areas; beach access is public

## Family-Friendly Attractions

### Theme Parks
- **SeaWorld San Diego**
  - Admission: $89-109 (varies by season)
  - Marine shows, rides, and animal encounters
  - Educational programs and conservation focus
  - Seasonal events and shows

- **LEGOLAND California**
  - Admission: $89-105 (varies by season)
  - Ideal for children ages 2-12
  - 60+ rides, shows, and attractions
  - Water park and SEA LIFE Aquarium included

### Educational Attractions
- **Birch Aquarium at Scripps**
  - Admission: Adults $24.50, Children $19.50
  - Hours: 9am-5pm daily
  - Interactive marine exhibits
  - Touch tanks and tide pool displays
  - Ocean conservation education

### Outdoor Activities
- **Mission Bay Park**
  - Free admission
  - 4,600 acres of water and parkland
  - Bike paths, playgrounds, and beaches
  - Water sports rentals available
  - Perfect for picnics and family activities

## Must-See Locations for Visitors

### Natural Wonders
- **Torrey Pines State Natural Reserve**
  - Location: 12600 N Torrey Pines Rd, La Jolla, CA 92037
  - Admission: $12-25 per vehicle
  - Hours: 7:15am-sunset daily
  - Highlights:
    - Rare Torrey pine trees
    - Hiking trails with ocean views
    - Unspoiled beach access
    - Bird watching and wildlife

- **Coronado Island**
  - Free bridge access
  - Beautiful beaches and charming downtown
  - Hotel del Coronado
  - Ferry service from downtown
  - Bike rentals and island tours

### Cultural Districts
- **San Diego's Art & Culture Scene**
  - Balboa Park museums (many free on Tuesdays)
  - North Park and South Park arts districts
  - Public art installations throughout the city
  - Monthly art walks and gallery openings

### Scenic Drives
- **59-Mile Scenic Drive**
  - Self-guided tour of San Diego highlights
  - Coastal routes with ocean views
  - Covers major attractions and neighborhoods
  - Perfect for orientation and sightseeing

## Practical Information

### Transportation
- **Public Transit**: San Diego Trolley and bus system
- **Rideshare**: Uber and Lyft widely available
- **Car Rentals**: Available at airport and city locations
- **Biking**: Bike-friendly city with many rental options

### Best Times to Visit
- **Spring (March-May)**: Pleasant weather, fewer crowds
- **Fall (September-November)**: Warm weather, summer crowds gone
- **Winter**: Mild temperatures, whale watching season
- **Summer**: Peak tourist season, warmest weather

### Money-Saving Tips
- **Free Museum Tuesdays**: Many Balboa Park museums offer free admission
- **CityPASS**: Discounted combo tickets for multiple attractions
- **Military Discounts**: Available at many attractions
- **Local Resident Deals**: Check websites for San Diego resident specials

## Seasonal Highlights

### Summer (June-August)
- Beach activities and water sports
- Outdoor concerts and events
- Balboa Park summer programs
- Perfect for family vacations

### Fall (September-November)
- Whale watching season begins
- fewer crowds
- Pleasant temperatures
- Halloween events and activities

### Winter (December-February)
- Gray whale migration
- Holiday events and decorations
- Fewer crowds
- Mild weather for outdoor activities

### Spring (March-May)
- Wildflower blooms
- Spring break activities
- Perfect weather for outdoor exploration
- Festival season begins
